# MOT And Customer Journey

Use this reference when the task is about customer journey, service design, first purchase, repeat purchase, referral, retention, MOT, peak experience, or brand moments.

## MOT Purpose

- MOT should generate behavior and implant memory.
- Different MOTs can be weighted according to company strategy.
- Review the exact moment where a user can change behavior, not the whole journey in the abstract.

Source cards: `A5A1`, `A`.

## Golden Moments

Prioritize:

1. First moment
2. Highest or peak moment
3. Final moment

Source card: `A5A10`.

## Peak-End Rule

- Remembered experience is strongly shaped by the peak and ending.
- Other parts still matter, but redesign should first check whether the peak and ending are weak, confusing, or emotionally flat.
- Repeat purchase often depends on whether the peak experience feels meaningfully different.

Source cards: `A5A21`, `A5A19`.

## First Purchase / First Use Questions

For first-order or first-use review, ask:

1. Does the expectation before the experience match the actual experience?
2. Where is the peak?
3. Where is the low point?
4. What memory mark is left?
5. What makes the person want the next purchase or next use?
6. How does the experience differ by segment?

Source card: `A5A16`.

## Sensory Cues

- The more relevant senses an experience engages, the easier it is to make the moment concrete and memorable.
- Use sensory cues only when they reinforce the desired behavior or memory.
- Avoid adding sensory details that distract from the core task or trust signal.

Source card: `A5A1B`.

## Journey Completeness

- In a customer stage, help the customer receive a complete product experience, not a disconnected touchpoint.
- Marketing experience should appear at the relevant journey stage.
- Strong customer experience can outperform more promotion when the moment itself becomes persuasive.

Source cards: `A2E2`, `A2E3`, `A4F5`.

## Referral Trigger

- Referral often needs emotional impulse near the end of service.
- Ask what the customer liked most, what changed afterward, and whether someone around them may need the same help.

Source card: `A1A1B1A1A`.
