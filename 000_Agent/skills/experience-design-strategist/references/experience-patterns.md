# Experience Patterns

Use this reference for experience design book concepts: intuitive action, story, empathy, growth, tension, repetition, and emotional memory.

## Problem Type To Strategy

| Problem Type | Strategy | Review Question |
|---|---|---|
| Too complex | Make it intuitive | Can the person know what to do without explanation? |
| Too frustrating | Add surprise, relief, rhythm, or feedback | Does the experience change emotional texture at the right moment? |
| No achievement | Add story, progress, difficulty choice, or growth | Can the person feel progress or transformation? |

Source cards: `D1`, `D1B`.

## Empathy Through A Problem

- Start with a problem the user or protagonist faces.
- Make the audience stand on the same side as the protagonist before asking them to care.
- Let the painful object or obstacle create shared feeling, not decorative drama.

Source card: `D1A`.

## Repetition And Growth

- A repeatable experience needs intuitive action, continuing interest, and a reason to come back.
- Difficulty choice plus corresponding feedback creates agency and growth.
- Leaving intentional gaps for repeated action can let people improve without feeling like they are being taught.
- The first successful experience can reduce later fear and make harder future challenges easier to approach.

Source cards: `D1B`, `M1`, `DZ6A`, `A1A1G1A`.

## Tension, Gaps, And Continuity

- Unresolved questions can sustain attention through the Zeigarnik effect.
- Do not leave empty space between important questions in a guided experience.
- Let people fill meaningful gaps; filling a gap can create a feeling of growth.
- Alternate information density and active/passive moments to create rhythm.

Source cards: `C3B1A1B1`, `C3B1A1B1A`, `C3B1A1B1B`, `C3B5A`.

## Emotional Memory

- Experiences become long-term memory through emotion.
- Design for a specific remembered feeling: relief, confidence, surprise, recognition, achievement, belonging, or trust.
- Strong emotions increase influence, so use them carefully and tie them to real value.

Source cards: `Q1A`, `C4A`.

## Story And Connection

- Story can help people understand complex things with less pain.
- Good experience stories often contain a goal, obstacle, effort, result, surprise, turn, and ending.
- Shared story and emotional connection can give a brand or experience more binding power than feature explanation alone.

Source cards: `C3`, `C3A1`, `C3B1`, `C3G2A`.
