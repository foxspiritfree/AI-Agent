# Scoring Rules

## Candidate Signals

Score candidates by these signals:

- External demand: visible interest or recurring theme in hot posts.
- Personal signal: the user has an experience, prior post, journal note, or clear stance.
- Identity fit: the topic connects naturally to the user's stable writing identity and core themes.
- Knowledge support: study card or durable concept can deepen the piece.
- Voice fit: the topic can be written in the user's normal rhythm and judgment frame.
- Freshness: not too similar to recent drafts or posts.

## Classes

### A: Ready To Draft

Use when:

- personal signal exists
- identity fit is natural
- knowledge support exists or is not needed
- external demand is clear enough
- voice fit is natural

### B: Needs User Experience

Use when:

- angle is promising
- external or knowledge signal exists
- personal signal is missing or too thin
- identity fit is plausible once the user adds a concrete experience or stance

Ask for one concrete experience or one turn in thinking.

### C: Hot But Not Yet You

Use when:

- external demand exists
- identity fit or voice fit is weak
- no personal signal is found

Do not draft from C by default.

## Revival Rule

B and C can become draftable if the user adds:

- a real experience
- a clear stance
- a before/after turn
- a reason they disagree with the obvious popular angle

After revival, preserve the user's new personal signal as the center of the draft and check that it now connects to an identity theme rather than only a trend.

## Hard Stops

Do not draft if:

- the topic is only a trend with no user signal
- the topic cannot connect to a concrete identity theme without forcing it
- the draft would require pretending the user has an experience they did not provide
- the strongest version would sound like generic advice
- the user explicitly says they do not want that angle
